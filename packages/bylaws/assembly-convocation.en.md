# Convocation Rules of the Agentic Organisations Collective

> **Non-binding English translation.** This is a courtesy translation of the German
> convocation rules ([`assembly-convocation.md`](assembly-convocation.md)) for
> members and interested persons. Only the German version (*Einberufungsordnung*) is
> legally binding. In the event of any discrepancy between the two versions, the
> German text prevails. References to German statutes are retained because they
> designate the applicable German law.

These convocation rules supplement the statutes of the Agentic Organisations
Collective. They govern how the general assembly is prepared and convened, based on
§ 8 of the statutes. They are not part of the statutes and may be amended by a simple
majority.

> **Status: draft.** These rules are to be adopted by the competent body after the
> association is founded (see § 6). Until then they serve as the intended procedure.

## § 1 Purpose and scope

(1) These rules describe how the board prepares and convenes the general assembly.
The goal is the highest possible member turnout while reliably meeting the statutory
deadlines.

(2) The provisions of the statutes always take precedence over these rules.

## § 2 Observing the notice period

(1) The formal convocation is made under § 8 (2) of the statutes **in text form,
stating the agenda, with a notice period of two weeks**. This period is unaffected
by the procedure in § 3 and must not be shortened by it.

(2) Any preliminary date-finding procedure (§ 3) must be planned so that, once
concluded, the formal convocation for the chosen date can still be issued within the
required notice period.

## § 3 Four-step procedure

The board normally prepares an ordinary general assembly as follows:

1. **Announcement of planning.** The board announces the planned assembly and the
   rough topics in text form.
2. **Date-finding.** The board offers several proposed dates via a
   privacy-friendly polling tool (§ 4) and asks members to keep as many options open
   as possible to achieve broad participation. **All proposed dates must lie far
   enough in the future that, after the poll concludes, the formal convocation still
   observes the two-week notice period under § 2.** As a guideline, the earliest
   proposed date is at least three weeks after the poll opens (roughly one week of
   polling plus two weeks of notice).
3. **Topic collection.** During or after date-finding, the board invites members to
   submit further agenda items and prepares the agenda.
4. **Formal invitation.** The board convenes the assembly with the fixed date and the
   final agenda in text form, observing the notice period under § 2.

## § 4 Polling tool and data protection

(1) The tool used for date-finding must be data-protection compliant and should be
usable without a mandatory member user account.

(2) Currently **Nuudel** (operated by Digitalcourage e. V., EU) is used for this.
The board decides which tool is used; the currently valid choice is documented in the
members' area (see the "Tooling & ways of working" overview in the members
repository).

## § 5 Form of the assembly and voting

The assembly may be held as an in-person, virtual, or hybrid assembly under § 8 (3)
of the statutes; voting may also be electronic. Resolutions by written or electronic
circular procedure under § 8 (5) of the statutes remain unaffected.

## § 6 Entry into force

These convocation rules were adopted by the board on [insert date] and enter into
force with that resolution. The general assembly may confirm or amend them.
