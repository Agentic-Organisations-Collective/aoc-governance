# Membership Admission Rules of the Agentic Organisations Collective

> **Non-binding English translation.** This is a courtesy translation of the
> German membership admission rules
> ([`member-admission.md`](member-admission.md)) for members and interested
> persons. Only the German version (*Aufnahmeordnung*) is legally binding. In the
> event of any discrepancy between the two versions, the German text prevails.
> References to German statutes are retained because they designate the
> applicable German law.

These membership admission rules supplement the statutes of the Agentic
Organisations Collective. They govern the procedure for admitting new members on
the basis of § 4 of the statutes. They are not part of the statutes and may be
amended by simple majority.

> **Status: Draft.** These rules are adopted by the board (see § 8). Until then
> they serve as the intended procedure.

## § 1 Purpose and Scope

(1) These rules describe how admission of new members is decided and how the
admission process runs, from application through to admission into the working
and communication spaces of the association.

(2) Under § 4 (5) of the statutes, the board decides on admission. There is no
entitlement to admission.

(3) The provisions of the statutes always take precedence over these rules.

## § 2 Admission Requirements

The admission requirements and the forms of membership are governed by § 4 of the
statutes. Accordingly, ordinary membership requires a professional or practical
connection to the association's field of activity as well as the willingness to
engage in open, respectful cooperation (§ 4 (4)); it exists as individual or
organisational membership (§ 4 (2)), and supporting membership is also possible
(§ 4 (3)). These rules apply to both forms accordingly.

## § 3 Application

(1) The application for admission must be made in text form, in accordance with
§ 4 (5) of the statutes.

(2) The application should contain the information required for review and
administration, in particular:

- name and contact details of the applicant,
- the desired type of membership (ordinary individual or organisational
  membership, or supporting membership) and, for organisational membership, the
  designated natural person,
- information on the professional or practical connection to the association's
  field of activity,
- for organisations, the information required for fee classification under the
  contribution schedule,
- a statement regarding publication of the membership under § 4 (7) of the
  statutes.

(3) If information essential to the decision is missing, the board may request
completion before deciding.

## § 4 Procedure

The board generally handles an incoming application as follows:

1. **Receipt and preparation.** The application is recorded and prepared for the
   decision; the details are checked for completeness and against the
   requirements under § 2.
2. **Board deliberation.** The application is submitted to the board for review
   and deliberation.
3. **Decision.** The board decides on admission (§ 5).
4. **Documentation.** The decision is recorded in a traceable manner; the member
   data is entered into the member registry (§ 7).
5. **Notification.** The decision is communicated to the applicant (§ 5).
6. **Contribution.** The membership fee is governed by the contribution schedule.
7. **Admission to the working and communication spaces.** After admission, the
   member is added to the working and communication spaces intended for members.

## § 5 Decision and Notification

(1) The decision is taken by the board by simple majority (§ 9 (4) of the
statutes); it should generally be taken within four weeks of receipt of a
complete application.

(2) A rejection does not require reasons (§ 4 (5) of the statutes). The
association treats applicants with appreciation even in the event of a rejection.

(3) The decision is communicated to the applicant in text form.

## § 6 Beginning of Membership

Membership begins when the admission notification reaches the applicant. Rights
and obligations arising from membership, in particular the obligation to pay dues
under the contribution schedule, arise at this point in time, unless the
contribution schedule provides otherwise.

## § 7 Documentation, Procedural Support and Data Protection

(1) The admission process and the admission decision are documented in a
traceable manner. The member registry is kept as a confidential record reserved
to the board.

(2) Personal data of applicants and members is collected, processed and stored
confidentially only to the extent necessary for member administration.

(3) The board may support the procedure with suitable tools and automation, in
particular for recording, preparation, documentation and execution. The decision
on admission is always taken by the board (§ 4 (5) of the statutes).

## § 8 Entry into Force

enter into force upon that resolution. The general assembly may confirm or amend
them.
